# Buenos Aires Program — Brand Identity Guide

**A Pepperdine University International Program**

-----

## 00 / Brand Context

### A Program within a University

Pepperdine operates a master brand strategy: all constituents fall under the Pepperdine umbrella. The Buenos Aires Program honors this relationship while developing a visual identity suited to its distinct context, audience, and location.

> **The guiding principle:** The BAP is unmistakably Pepperdine in its foundations (typography, institutional tone, brand architecture) while cultivating a lighter, more internationally minded palette drawn from its own logo. When materials will carry the Pepperdine seal or appear alongside other University programs, lean toward the parent system. When materials are specific to the Buenos Aires experience, the BAP palette takes the lead.

## 01 / Color Palette

The BAP logo centers on a single, luminous blue (`#64B5F6`) paired with white and black. This blue sits close to Pepperdine’s secondary “Sky” tone (Pantone 2915), creating a natural bridge to the University palette while giving the program a lighter, more open feel.

### BAP Logo Colors

**Buenos Aires Blue** — Primary

- HEX `#64B5F6`
- RGB 100, 181, 246
- HSL 207°, 89%, 68%
- *Use for:* backgrounds, accents, icons, primary brand surfaces

**White**

- HEX `#FFFFFF`
- *Use for:* logo band, backgrounds, negative space

**Black**

- HEX `#000000`
- *Use for:* logo illustration detail

### A Note on Orange

The BAP logo intentionally omits Pepperdine Orange, opting for a cooler, more cosmopolitan palette. However, Pepperdine’s guidelines indicate that blue and orange should appear in some context in every official communication. For co-branded or University-facing materials, consider a small orange accent (a rule line, a button, a tagline highlight) to satisfy this requirement without disrupting the BAP’s visual identity.

## 02 / Typography

The BAP aligns with Pepperdine’s type strategy: open-source Google Fonts designed for both print and digital. The University recommends Roboto for general use and EB Garamond when a serif is appropriate. A monospace utility face rounds out the system for labels and data.

### Typeface Inventory

**EB Garamond** — Display / Headlines

- Source: Pepperdine recommended serif
- Available: [Google Fonts](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/EB+Garamond)
- Classic, high-contrast serif. Use at large sizes for titles, headings, and pull quotes.

**Roboto** — Body / Print / Digital

- Source: Pepperdine recommended sans-serif
- Available: [Google Fonts](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Roboto)
- Clean, contemporary, and versatile across all contexts. Pepperdine’s primary general-use typeface.

**PT Sans** — Web Primary

- Source: Pepperdine designated web typeface
- Available: [Google Fonts](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/PT+Sans)
- For BAP web properties within the University’s CMS or matching Pepperdine’s web presence, use PT Sans. For standalone BAP sites or applications, Roboto is also acceptable.

**DM Mono** — Labels / Data

- Source: BAP addition (not part of Pepperdine system)
- Available: [Google Fonts](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/DM+Mono)
- Utility monospace for captions, metadata, dates, and technical labels in BAP-specific materials.

## 03 / Color in Use

### Approved Combinations

These pairings cover both BAP-specific contexts and co-branded scenarios that need to acknowledge the University palette.

**Formal** — Pep Blue background + white text + BAP Blue accents

- Best for: University-facing documents, official correspondence, co-branded flyers

**BAP Primary** — BAP Blue background + white text + Pep Blue accents

- Best for: program-specific social media, student-facing materials, event signage

**Light** — White background + Pep Blue headlines + BAP Blue subheads

- Best for: letterhead, reports, print documents, email headers

**Soft** — Ice Blue background + Pep Blue headlines + Ocean accents

- Best for: informational handouts, course materials, internal documents

**Co-Brand** — Pep Blue background + white text + Orange accent

- Best for: materials alongside other Pepperdine programs, alumni communications

**Neutral** — Parchment background + Mountain text + Pep Blue headlines

- Best for: invitations, contemplative or editorial-style layouts

## 04 / Voice & Tone

Pepperdine’s brand promise centers on educating heart, soul, and mind. The BAP carries this forward with the additional dimension of cross-cultural immersion in Argentina.

### Warm but Not Casual

Write with the voice of a knowledgeable guide who genuinely cares about each student’s experience. Be approachable without being flippant; authoritative without being stiff.

*Keywords:* welcoming, informed, culturally attentive, encouraging

### Adventurous but Grounded

Convey the excitement of discovery and the richness of Argentine culture, while maintaining the program’s academic seriousness and Pepperdine’s institutional credibility.

*Keywords:* aspirational, reflective, transformative, purposeful

### Bilingual Sensibility

Naturally weave in Spanish (using Argentine forms) where appropriate. The program lives in two languages; the brand should feel comfortable in both without over-translating or code-switching awkwardly.

*Keywords:* bicultural, locally rooted, globally aware, *vos* over *tú*

### Mission-Aligned

Pepperdine’s brand promise emphasizes that every touchpoint communicates and reinforces the institution’s values. BAP materials should reflect this: study abroad is not merely academic tourism but a formative, purpose-driven chapter of a student’s education.

*Keywords:* faith-informed, service-oriented, character-forming, community-centered

## 05 / Logo & Brand Usage

### Do

- Use the full-color logo on white or very light backgrounds
- Maintain clear space around the logo equal to at least the height of the “EST” text
- Use Roboto and EB Garamond to stay aligned with Pepperdine’s type system
- Include a small Pepperdine Orange element in University-facing or co-branded materials
- Use “Buenos Aires Program” as the primary name; “BAP” for internal shorthand only
- Let the logo’s circular form breathe with generous margins
- Reference the Pepperdine seal or wordmark when institutional context is needed

### Don’t

- Place the logo on busy photographic backgrounds without a white container
- Stretch, rotate, or alter the logo proportions
- Use colors outside both the BAP and Pepperdine palettes for branded materials
- Recreate or approximate the logo with substitute typefaces
- Use “BAP” in external-facing, parent-facing, or University communications
- Crop into the circular logo or remove the outer ring
- Pair the BAP logo with the Pepperdine seal at the same visual weight; one should be primary
